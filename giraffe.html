GIRAFFE

Giraffes are the world's tallest mammals, known for their long necks and distinctive spotted coat patterns. They are native to the savannas of Africa and are social animals that often gather in groups.

The long neck of a giraffe, which can reach up to 18 feet (5.5 meters), allows them to reach leaves high in trees that other animals cannot access. Their tongue is also prehensile, enabling them to grasp and pull leaves from branches.

Giraffes are herbivores, mainly feeding on leaves, fruits, and flowers. Their unique spotted coat acts as a form of camouflage in the wild, helping them blend into the patterns of light and shadow in their habitat.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

You can spot giraffes in the African Savannah exhibit, enjoying the tall trees and grazing on the abundant vegetation.
