WARTHOG

Warthogs are medium-sized wild pigs known for their distinctive appearance with warty protrusions on their faces. They are found in various habitats across sub-Saharan Africa.

Warthogs are herbivores and primarily graze on grasses, roots, and tubers. They use their strong snouts to dig for food and create burrows where they rest and rear their young.

Despite their tough appearance, warthogs are generally non-aggressive and rely on their keen senses to detect predators. They are social animals and often form groups called sounders.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

Observe the warthogs in their habitat near the Savannah Zone, where they can be seen foraging and interacting with each other.
